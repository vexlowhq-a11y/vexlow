Synthesia Expands AI Training with Interactive Roleplay Sessions

The company introduces a new platform for enterprise training that allows employees to practice conversations with AI avatars for enhanced feedback and analytics.

VexlowHQ Staff·July 22, 2026·3 min

Synthesia, known for its AI video production technology, has announced a groundbreaking development in its training resources. The company has launched AI Roleplay Sessions, an innovative platform designed for interactive enterprise training. This new system enables employees to engage in simulated workplace conversations with AI-generated avatars, providing immediate feedback, scoring, and detailed analytics.

The AI Roleplay Sessions aim to enhance employee training by creating realistic scenarios that simulate common workplace interactions. By doing so, organizations can practice communications ranging from customer service to team collaboration in a controlled, feedback-rich environment.

Enhanced Feedback and Analytics

This shift from traditional video content to live coaching signifies a broader trend in corporate training, focusing on immersive learning experiences. Synthesia's platform not only facilitates practice but also quantifies training effectiveness through analytics. Companies will be able to evaluate how well their employees are performing in real-time, which can help identify strengths and areas needing improvement.

Moreover, the interactive nature of the platform allows for a tailored training experience. Employees can rehearse various scenarios, receive constructive feedback, and adjust their approach before interacting with real colleagues or clients. This could ultimately lead to more confident and effective communication skills in the workplace.

Implications for Corporate Training

The introduction of AI Roleplay Sessions highlights a significant movement toward integrating artificial intelligence into professional development. By leveraging technology in this manner, businesses are likely to see improvements in overall employee engagement and skill acquisition. As firms increasingly adopt AI-driven learning tools, the demand for traditional training methods may decrease.

In an era where remote work and hybrid teams are becoming more common, having access to such advanced training solutions could be an essential factor for companies striving to maintain performance and productivity.

This development may signal a shift in how organizations approach employee training, making it more personalized and dynamic, aligning with the fast-paced demands of today's business environment.
